Significant prior work on the so-called ‘depth-charge illusion’ attests that sentences such as ‘No head injury is too trivial to be ignored’ tend to receive what is seen as a compositionally unavailable reading (e.g. Kizach et al., 2016; Paape et al., 2020; Wason & Reich, 1979). Based on the semantics of degrees, we can, however, consider the possibility that the commonly received interpretation may be semantically available, in alignment with proposals characterising such sentences as structurally ambiguous (e.g. Fortuin, 2014). This talk presents work in progress: I build on concepts in degree semantics in order to introduce an analysis of depth-charge sentences as developed by Hohaus and Bade (2022), and discuss a potential experimental approach to test some of its predictions.

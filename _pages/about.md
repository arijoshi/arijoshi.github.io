I'm an aspiring linguist, primarily interested in theory-driven experimental and computational work at the nexus of semantics, pragmatics, and psycholinguistics. From July 2024, I will join the collaborative LMBayes project at ZAS Berlin (in collaboration with University of Tuebingen & WIAS Berlin) as a doctoral researcher, working on modelling individual differences in the interpretation of quantifiers. 

Currently, I'm writing up my MSc thesis at the University of Potsdam, where I'm also an RA in Malte Zimmermann's project in our collaborative research center (SFB 1287) on Variability in Language. I also work at ZAS Berlin in the Semantics & Pragmatics department, mainly assisting Andreea Cristina Nicolae, and previously, Aron Hirsch.

Outside of academic work, I love solving massive jigsaw puzzles, doing crosswords and word riddles, learning new languages just up to the point where it starts getting serious, writing code for stupid tasks, reading mostly fiction and sometimes pop-science, watching very long video essays, playing board games, doing escape rooms, and cuddling the furry beings who live with me. I have a special affinity to very, very silly humour. 

ongoing projects
======
- can so-called 'depth-charge' sentences be analysed semantically? [MSc thesis, supervised by Malte Zimmermann and Nadine Bade]
- how flexible are people in interpreting gradable adjectives, specifically colour terms?
- [joint work with Aron Hirsch] different formal analyses of 'only' make divergent predictions about the processing speed of 'only' at different positions. can we use these predictions to find out which analysis best captures the usage?

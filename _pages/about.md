I'm an MSc student at the University of Potsdam, where I'm also an RA in Malte Zimmermann's project in our collaborative research center. I also work at ZAS Berlin, mainly with Andreea Cristina Nicolae and previously with Aron Hirsch. I'm primarily interested in theory-driven experimental work at the nexus of semantics, pragmatics, and psycholinguistics. From July 2024, I will join the collaborative LMBayes project at ZAS Berlin as a doctoral researcher, working on modelling individual differences in the interpretation of quantifiers. 

Outside of academic work, I love solving massive jigsaw puzzles, doing crosswords and word riddles, learning new languages to a moderate level, writing code for stupid tasks, reading both fiction and non-fiction, watching very long video essays on random topics, playing board games or doing escape rooms with my partner, and cuddling the two furry beings who live with me. You'd often see me laughing at the dumbest, lamest humour you can imagine.

ongoing projects
======
- can so-called 'depth-charge' sentences be explained semantically? [MSc dissertation, supervised by Malte Zimmermann and Nadine Bade]
- how flexible are people in interpreting gradable adjectives, specifically colour terms?
- [assisting Andreea Cristina Nicolae & Yasutada Sudo]: there are multiple ways of expressing disjunction; do they vary systematically in terms of inferences that can be derived? 
- [joint work with Aron Hirsch] different formal analyses of 'only' make divergent predictions about the processing speed of 'only' at different positions. can we use this to make a choice?
